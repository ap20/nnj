The United States Energy Situation

Undoubtedly we all have heard pronouncements about how states are trying to set goals for a more diversified energy economy. Often such discussions gravitate around increased use of alternate/renewable energy options. Many states and their utility regulatory organizations want to have solar/wind technologies contributing 50% or more of their electrical energy needs—usually quoting target dates ranging from 2025 to 2050.

These are challenging goals, often entailing big assumptions and incentives for installing alternate/renewable systems. Historical experience indicates it can take 30-60 years or more to significantly change the energy economy.

Below are two tables about our nation’s energy mix you might find interesting. Draw your own conclusions about whether those lofty alternate/renewable goals are achievable.

U.S. Energy Mix 1910-2017 [in quads–a quadrillion= (10 to the 15th) BTU]

Oil Natural Gas Coal Nuclear Hydro Wood/Biomass Solar /Wind
2017 36 28 14 8 3 5 3
2005 40 22 23 8 3 3 1
1950 12 6 13 0 2 0 0
1910 0.7 0.3 10.9 0 0.3 1.8 0

Notice the:

  • Drop in petroleum use
  • Steady increase in use of natural gas
  • Drop in use of coal
  • Increased use of renewables

Fuel Mix of U.S. Electric Utility Sector [numbers presented in % of usage]
