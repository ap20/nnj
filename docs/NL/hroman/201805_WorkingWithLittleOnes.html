Working with the Little Ones

For about 50 years now, I have enjoyed working with teachers at various schools to bring the excitement of science and technology into the classroom. Since retiring about 12 years ago, I have been doing many sci-tech demos at the Thomas Edison National Historical Park in West Orange where I am a docent and sort of resident inventor. I also do my demos at several local schools in West Orange and Montclair. Hands down, the best audiences are the young ones... grades 3 thru 6.

I guess it is the sparkle in their eyes that fascinates me, the moment when everything clicks, and they understand. It is so honest and real as that huge smile comes to their lips. They almost seem to vibrate with excitement.

A favorite among my many demo activities involves making wet cell batteries... two dissimilar metals, safe salt water as the electrolyte, a simple voltmeter and the kids respond like crazy. They instantly start experimenting with trying to hook multiple cells together, soon discovering series circuits. Then they start trying different metal pairs. Parallel circuits elude them at first, but a little explanation and they get the message.

Been working on something really cool for them... an eye-popper... using Play Doh as a conductor of electricity and lighting up some LEDS. But I have something more powerful in store for them... Play Doh wet cells... no liquid needed... just put the metals in and read a voltage. Boy are they going to smile at that one. Got all these demos out on my big basement workshop table ready to go. Mama bought me some serious containers of Play Doh, and I plan to use them all!

Maybe you have had the pleasure of working with the young ones too. If you have, you know the incredible questions they can ask. I encourage you to spend some time in the classroom. It is highly rewarding and so important for students to see role models who can talk and do sci-tech. I recently sat on a panel at a science day celebration at a Montclair elementary school -- about 12 panels were in the school visiting the children in grades 3 thru 5. That old school was pulsating with excitement as the young students learned how all those science visitors had started their careers and what they do every day on the job. Amazing stuff for young students to hear.

There is another benefit from working with teachers and young students. You will feel young again. And it's OK to take a little nap when you get back home. I do so all the time. All that fun will tire you out!

Talk to you again soon...
