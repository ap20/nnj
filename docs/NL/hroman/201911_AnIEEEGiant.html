An IEEE Giant

Let's see if you can identify this IEEE giant.

At Edison's legendary West Orange labs, this Canadian-born inventor worked for about three years on a broad range of problems in chemistry, metallurgy, and electricity. Edison valued him for his ability to work out solutions across a variety of technical areas; and able to think on his feet. His initials... "RAF".

Here is a list of his "creds"... maybe this will help you identify him:

  • Held 500+ patents
  • Considered to be the Father of Radio-telephony
  • Taught electrical engineering at Purdue
  • Discovered the principle of amplitude modulation-leading to AM radio (heterodyne receivers)
  • Made the first audio radio transmission in 1900; music transmission in 1906
  • Helped engineer the Niagara Falls power plant
  • Helped install the lighting for the 1893 Chicago World Columbian Exposition
  • Developed sonar systems to determine ocean depth, iceberg collision avoidance, and undersea communication
  • Developed seismic reflection principles used for land-based oil exploration
  • Received patents for tracer bullets, microfilm, television apparatus, turbo electric drive for ships, and more.

So... any idea who this great inventor was ? It was Reginald Aubrey Fessenden.

One final note, Like Edison, he also had a WWII destroyer named after him, DE-142, the USS Fessenden.
